# Facebook Platform Rules

Layers on top of `SHARED.md`. Target: the **MZS Networks Facebook page** (a brand page, not a personal timeline).

## Format

- **Length:** shorter than LinkedIn, longer than a caption. **Sweet spot: ~400–800 characters.** Facebook's hard cap is effectively unlimited (63k), but engagement falls off fast past ~1,000.
- **The fold:** Facebook truncates at ~477 characters on desktop (~400 mobile) behind "See more." Hook *and* the core claim must land above it.
- **Structure:** hook line → POV → one load-bearing cited stat → judgment → landing/question.
- **Paragraphs:** short, one to two lines, generous white space — same as LinkedIn but tighter.

## Voice

- Slightly warmer than LinkedIn, still an engineer talking. Personal framing works well ("I watched this happen on a Monday morning").
- One idea per post. Facebook rewards the conversation a post starts more than its completeness — end on a real question when the idea supports one.

## Emoji

- Sparing. One or two on key beats is fine; more reads as a meme page. Between LinkedIn (~none) and Instagram (purposeful, heavier).

## Hashtags

- **0–2.** Hashtags do almost nothing for reach on Facebook; include them only when they're natural (`#NetDevOps`). Zero is a valid choice.

## Links & Sources

- Skip the full `## Sources` block — it reads as clutter here. Cite inline ("per Gartner, 2025").
- If the post needs a link, pass it as the post's **link attachment** (renders a preview card), not pasted mid-text. Know that Facebook deprioritizes outbound links in organic reach — only attach one when it earns its cost.

## Media

- A visual (infographic or carousel cover) markedly outperforms text-only on Facebook. When the idea folder has one, attach it.

## Calibration

Render from the same master as the other platforms: keep the spine (hook → POV → cited data → judgment → landing), compress toward the Instagram end of the range, keep the LinkedIn seriousness.
