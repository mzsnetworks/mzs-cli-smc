NetDevOps doesn't fail because the tooling is bad. It fails because the wrong people own it. 🛠️

The common pattern: a separate team builds the pipelines, then hands them to operations to run.

And it slowly rots.

Not because ops is resistant. Because the people who wrote it don't carry its consequences:
→ they don't get the 3am page
→ they didn't know about the load-bearing "temporary" workaround
→ they encoded the network they were told about, not the one ops runs

Automation written by people who don't operate the network is a set of assumptions someone else has to live with.

It works when operations owns it ⚙️ Not "is consulted." Owns it — writes it, breaks it, carries the pager. The moment the author and the operator are the same person, it all aligns: done matches reality, edge cases are known, trust is just self-interest.

The org chart matters more than the tool choice.

Give the automation to the people who get paged. That's the whole strategy.

Who owns your automation — the people who wrote it, or the people who live with it? 👇
