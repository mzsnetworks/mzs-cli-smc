"Plug-and-play" Wi-Fi is the most expensive lie in enterprise networking. 📡

You don't plug it in. You engineer it — or you reopen the same ticket forever.

The AP ships looking like an appliance. Mount it, power it, broadcast an SSID, done. In an empty room, it works. The demo always works.

Then Monday shows up with 200 clients, and the illusion dies. ⚠️

The problem isn't the AP. It never is. The AP is the one thing in wireless you actually control. What decides if Wi-Fi works lives outside the box 👇

→ Walls, people, the breakroom microwave
→ The neighbor's APs bleeding through the floor
→ Client radios that roam on their own decision, not yours

Most "Wi-Fi is slow" tickets aren't the AP. They're coverage holes, co-channel interference, or sticky clients clinging to a -80 dBm signal.

🏗️ The brownfield trap: every office that "fixed" coverage by adding more APs until channels collided learned it the hard way — more radios in 2.4 GHz is more interference, not more capacity.

Wired networks fail loudly. Wireless networks fail politely — a few users, some of the time, never on your dashboard.

Plug-and-play sells the access point. Engineering keeps the Wi-Fi. ⚖️ The clients decide who was right.
