You didn't adopt NetDevOps. You bought a pipeline and kept the same habits. 🔧

The quiet failure mode: a team stands up CI, puts configs in Git, declares the transformation done. The artifact is there. The culture never showed up.

NetDevOps was never the pipeline. The pipeline is what the culture produces — not what produces it.

The real shift is behavioral, and none of it ships in a tool:
→ changes reviewed before merge, by someone who'll say no
→ blameless postmortems, so near-misses surface instead of hiding
→ the network treated as a product, not a pile of devices
→ quality is shared — "it passed my change" stops being a defense

A pipeline without those habits just pushes the same unreviewed changes faster. You automated the mistake, not removed it ⚙️

The tooling is the easy 20%. The culture is the 80% no vendor can install for you.

Ask what happens when a change breaks production. If the answer is "we find who to blame," the pipeline is decoration.

What did your NetDevOps rollout actually change — the tooling, or how your team behaves when something breaks? 👇
