## Thread

1/ You don't have an alerting problem. You have a nobody-trusts-the-alerts problem. Alert fatigue is a trust failure.

2/ Engineers don't ignore alerts because they're tired. Experience taught them to: the CPU alert that clears itself, the disk warning firing daily since March. Ignoring became the correct move.

3/ Every false positive withdraws trust. At zero, the real alerting system goes informal: muted channels, mental lists, the engineer who just knows. It leaves when they do.

4/ The fix isn't fewer alerts — it's honest ones. Every alert names an action and an owner. If firing changes nothing in ten minutes, it's telemetry wearing a pager.

5/ Noise trains people to ignore. Ignoring trains incidents to grow. Trust is the only alerting metric that matters.

How many of your alerts would on-call bet a 2am wake-up on?
