## Thread

1/ IoT is the new brownfield. Ten-year devices, two-year vendor attention spans — and the network team inherits them when the pilot team disbands.

2/ How they enter: facilities pilots lighting, ops trials sensors, a vendor demos badge readers. 18 months later the pilot team is gone. 400 devices are still phoning home on day-one firmware.

3/ The trap: the device doesn't stop working. It keeps working — unpatched, unowned, on your network. Can't update it, can't justify replacing it, can't ignore it.

4/ Fix: treat IoT onboarding as brownfield intake. Before an IP — inventory entry, named owner, device-class segment, decommission plan. No answers = a liability with a radio.

5/ Greenfield is a design problem. Brownfield is an inheritance problem. IoT is inheritance at scale, arriving by the pallet.

Oldest device still phoning home to a vendor who stopped answering?
