IoT is the new brownfield. Every "smart" device is a ten-year liability with a two-year vendor attention span. 📡

How they enter: facilities pilots lighting, ops trials sensors, a vendor demos badge readers. Eighteen months later the pilot team is gone — and 400 devices are still on the network, running the firmware they shipped with.

The timelines cross: the building keeps the device a decade; the vendor's interest lasts two years. Firmware slows, the API sunsets, support becomes a KB article. The device keeps working — unpatched, unowned, on YOUR network. That's the trap.

The fix: treat IoT onboarding as brownfield intake. Before anything gets an IP — inventory entry, named owner, device-class segment, decommission plan. No answers? You're not deploying a device. You're adopting a liability with a radio. 🎒

Greenfield is a design problem. Brownfield is an inheritance problem. IoT is inheritance at scale — arriving by the pallet.
