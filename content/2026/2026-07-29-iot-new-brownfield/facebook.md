# Facebook — iot-new-brownfield (MZS page)

IoT is the new brownfield. Every "smart" device you onboard is a ten-year liability backed by a two-year vendor attention span — and the network team inherits it when the pilot team disbands.

The building runs those devices for a decade. The vendor's enthusiasm runs about two years: firmware slows, the cloud API sunsets, support becomes a knowledge-base article. The device keeps working — unpatched and unowned, on your network.

Treat IoT onboarding as brownfield intake: an inventory entry, a named owner, a segment, and a decommission plan — before anything gets an IP. Otherwise you're not deploying a device. You're adopting a liability with a radio.

What's the oldest device on your network still phoning home to a vendor who stopped answering? 👇
