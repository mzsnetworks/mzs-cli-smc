# Facebook — publish-ready

Your monitoring stack is a museum. It shows you what broke — beautifully, across forty dashboards nobody watches until the pager fires. 📉

Then it's 2am, and it tells you nothing you can act on.

Monitoring answers *what*. Observability answers *why*. Most teams bought the first, renamed it the second, and still wonder why outages take three hours.

Monitoring is the questions you knew to ask in advance — CPU, BGP up/down, a latency threshold. It only warns you about the failure you already imagined.

Observability is asking the question you *didn't* pre-plan, mid-outage. Can't do that on the spot? You don't have observability. You have graphs.

The tell is the war room: still SSH-ing into boxes for context the dashboards never caught? Those were monitoring. The green wall said the parts were up while the service was down.

Which one is your team actually running?
