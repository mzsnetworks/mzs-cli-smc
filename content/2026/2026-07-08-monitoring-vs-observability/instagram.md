## Caption

Your monitoring stack is a museum. It shows what broke — then tells you nothing you can act on at 2am. 📉

Monitoring answers *what*.
Observability answers *why*.

Most teams bought the first and renamed it the second 👇
→ Monitoring: the questions you knew to ask in advance (CPU, BGP, a latency threshold).
→ Observability: the question you *didn't* pre-plan, asked mid-outage, without shipping a new sensor.

The tell is the war room. Still SSH-ing into boxes for context the dashboards never caught? Those were monitoring. ⚙️

The green wall said the parts were up — while the service was down.

Monitoring tells you it's down.
Observability tells you why.
Judgment is knowing which one you bought.

Which one is your team running? 👇
