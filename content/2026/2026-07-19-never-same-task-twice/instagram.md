Automation's goal was never fewer engineers. It's engineers who never do the same task twice. 🔁

When the goal is fewer engineers, automation becomes a threat the team builds against itself. Nobody automates enthusiastically toward their own exit.

When the goal is never repeating a task, automation becomes compound learning: solve it once, encode it, move on. Every repetition you kill frees an engineer for the work that was never getting done.

The tell: headcount-driven teams automate what demos well. Repetition-driven teams automate what pages them. One produces impressive pilots. The other produces boring on-call — and boring is the goal. 😴

We've never seen well-run automation shrink a network team. Less typing, more designing. The engineers stayed. The repetition left.

Headcount is what finance counts. Toil is what engineers count. Automate the second — you'll never argue about the first.

What task has your team done 100 times that still isn't automated? 👇
