## Thread

1/ Automation's goal was never fewer engineers. It's engineers who never do the same task twice.

2/ Make the goal "fewer engineers" and automation becomes a threat the team builds against itself. Nobody automates enthusiastically toward their own exit.

3/ Make the goal "never repeat a task" and it becomes compound learning: solve once, encode, move on. Every repetition killed frees an engineer for work that was never getting done.

4/ The tell: headcount-driven teams automate what demos well. Repetition-driven teams automate what pages them. One gets impressive pilots. The other gets boring on-call — and boring is the goal.

5/ Headcount is what finance counts. Toil is what engineers count. Automate the second and you'll never argue about the first.

What task has your team done 100 times that still isn't automated?
