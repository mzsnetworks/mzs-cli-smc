Automation is a multiplier. Point it at a mess and all you get is a faster mess.

We reach for automation to fix inconsistency — but automation needs consistency to work. It assumes a pattern and applies it at scale. Feed it an environment where every switch was configured by a different engineer on a different Tuesday, and it reproduces the chaos, faster and in more places.

Ten snowflakes were manageable. Ten thousand, generated by a script, is a new class of problem.

Automation encodes assumptions that are true for some devices and quietly false for others — and the script can't tell. Now your outage isn't one misconfigured switch. It's every switch the job touched.

Standardize, then automate. Automation without it doesn't reduce your variance — it industrializes it.

What inconsistency would you fix before automating around it?
