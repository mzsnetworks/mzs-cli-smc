# Instagram Post — The Future of Network Engineering

## Caption

I've watched the industry argue NetOps vs NetDevOps for a decade. 🚪

Here's what I concluded after building in this space:

They're the same migration through different doors.
One protects the box.
The other treats the box as code.
Same destination — different suitcase.

The real question I care about 👇

What's a network engineer when the CLI isn't the job anymore?

The data answers it:
→ AI will write 25% of network configs by 2027 (was <3% in 2024)
→ 70% of network teams will lean on AI for daily ops
→ Automation adoption: 44% → 65% in two years

The engineer who memorized the syntax isn't being promoted.
They're being abstracted.

What's left? Judgment. ⚖️

When the machine writes the config, the rare skill isn't typing it — it's knowing when the AI is confidently wrong before it blackholes half your traffic.

The CLI made us operators.
Code made us engineers.
AI is about to make us judges.

When the machine writes the config — are you the one who can tell if it's right?
