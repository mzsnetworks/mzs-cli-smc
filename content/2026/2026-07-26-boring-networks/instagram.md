The best-run networks are boring. If your NOC is exciting, that's a symptom — not a badge of honor. 🧯

Excitement in operations is variance with good PR. Every "all hands on deck" means something got through: an unreviewed change, an untested failover, a workaround that finally collapsed.

Watch who gets celebrated: the all-nighter hero — applause. The engineer whose designs never went down — invisible. We promote firefighters and wonder why nobody invests in fire prevention.

The uncomfortable question: if your best engineer is always saving the day, why does the day keep needing to be saved? 🤔

A quiet network isn't lucky. It's designed. Reviewed changes, tested failovers, written-down intent, pruned alerts. No war stories. Just the job.

Heroics feel like performance. Operationally, they're rework in a costume.

Exciting NOCs make great stories. Boring NOCs make great networks. Decide which one you're building.

When was your last genuinely boring month? 👇
