## Thread

1/ The best-run networks are boring. If your NOC is exciting, that's a symptom — not a badge of honor.

2/ Excitement in operations is variance with good PR. Every "all hands on deck" means something got through: an unreviewed change, an untested failover, a collapsed workaround.

3/ Watch who gets celebrated: the all-nighter hero. Not the engineer whose designs never went down. We promote firefighters, then wonder why nobody invests in fire prevention.

4/ If your best engineer is always saving the day — why does the day keep needing to be saved?

5/ A quiet network isn't lucky. It's designed. Reviewed changes, tested failovers, written intent, pruned alerts. Heroics are rework in a costume.

6/ Exciting NOCs make great stories. Boring NOCs make great networks. Decide which one you're building.
