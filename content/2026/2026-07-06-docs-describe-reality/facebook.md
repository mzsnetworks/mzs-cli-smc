# Facebook — publish-ready (MZS Networks page)

The most dangerous document in your network isn't the one that's missing. It's the one that's confidently wrong.

Diagrams describe the day someone designed the network. Production starts diverging the week it goes live — the 11pm emergency change, the "temporary" VLAN from three years ago.

At 2am, wrong docs are worse than no docs. No docs, you verify everything. Wrong docs, you trust — and burn the first hour of the outage disproving your own diagram. 🔥

The fix isn't drawing discipline. It's documentation generated *from* the network, not written *about* it. Configs are evidence; diagrams are memories.

Intent lives in diagrams. Decisions live in configs. Truth lives only in the running network.
